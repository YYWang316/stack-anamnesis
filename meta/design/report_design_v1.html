Thesis & Framing

1.1 One-sentence thesis

USDC is Circle's attempt to be the regulated, audited default dollar of the on-chain economy — a compliance-first float business rather than a token; the key question is whether that regulatory and distribution moat can keep compounding supply share against USDT and bank-issued tokens once the interest-rate tailwind that funds the whole model fades.

USDC is the second-largest fiat-backed stablecoin and the reference "regulated dollar" on-chain, with ~$76.5B circulating (defillama, multi-chain, as_of 2026-05-28) and a ~$76.4B market cap that ranks #6 across all crypto assets (coingecko). It matters not as a price bet — the token trades at $0.9997 — but as the clearest proxy for whether regulated, transparently-reserved dollars are winning on-chain demand. Two pressures converge now: supply momentum is mixed-to-soft (−1.62% over 30d), and the issuer's reported FY2025 net income is negative (−$69.5M) on $2.75B revenue (sec_edgar) — a float business barely break-even at current rates is highly exposed to a Fed cutting cycle.
